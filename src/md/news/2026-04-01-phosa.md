    <p>
    The junior professorship for Computational Life Science and the
    Institute for Landscape Architecture launch a new, innovative learning
    format with the Politechnika Gdańsk (PG; Poland) and the ETH Zürich
    (Switzerland), following a successful application to the Enhance Testbed
    Seed Fund initiative.
    </p>
    <p>
      Climate change is one of the most pressing challenges of our time,
      particularly as cities face rising temperatures. Green facades offer a
      promising solution that combines plant science with architectural design.
    </p>
    <p>
    This interdisciplinary approach is at the core of the master's course
    PhoSA: Photosynthetic Systems in Architecture. Funded by the Testbed
    initiative of the Enhance Alliance, the course will include this year
    scenario-based hackathons in which RWTH students collaborate with peers
    (engineering and physics) from Politechnika Gdansk and ETH Zürich, who
    will visit during the summer semester, within a real-world
    client-consultant (company) framework.
    </p>
    <p>
    Students from Gdańsk and ETH collaborate with RWTH students to design
    and build a sensor system, while RWTH students develop and construct a
    vertical garden. This hands-on exchange strengthens both technical
    expertise and communication skills.
    </p>
